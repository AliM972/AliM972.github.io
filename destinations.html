Yosemite National Park:

Yosemite National Park in California, USA is one of the most iconic national parks in the United States. It covers an area of over 747,000 acres and is known for its breathtaking granite cliffs, waterfalls, and giant sequoia trees. Visitors can hike the park's many trails, including the famous Half Dome and Mist Trail, or climb the park's granite peaks. Yosemite is also home to a wide variety of wildlife, including black bears, coyotes, and mountain lions.

Banff National Park:

Banff National Park in Alberta, Canada is Canada's oldest national park and is located in the Rocky Mountains. It covers an area of over 2,500 square miles and is home to glaciers, mountains, lakes, and forests. Visitors can hike or bike the park's many trails, go skiing or snowboarding in the winter, or take a dip in the park's famous hot springs. Banff is also home to a variety of wildlife, including elk, bighorn sheep, and grizzly bears.

Lake District National Park:

Lake District National Park in England, UK is a mountainous region located in the north-west of England. It covers an area of 2,362 square kilometers and is home to sixteen major lakes and many smaller bodies of water. Visitors to the park can take scenic walks along the lakes or explore the park's many mountains and valleys. The Lake District is also famous for its literary connections, with poets such as William Wordsworth and Samuel Taylor Coleridge drawing inspiration from the area's natural beauty.
